Mediterranean Diet

Mediterranean food refers to the culinary traditions and dishes originating from the countries and regions surrounding the Mediterranean Sea. It encompasses a diverse range of cuisines influenced by countries such as Greece.

Benefits

The Mediterranean diet, which is renowned for its health benefits, forms the foundation of Mediterranean cuisine. It emphasizes the consumption of fresh, whole foods, including an abundance of fruits, vegetables, legumes, whole grains, nuts, and seeds. Olive oil, as a primary source of fat, is a key component, while lean proteins such as fish, poultry, and moderate amounts of dairy products are also incorporated.

Herbs and spices play a crucial role in Mediterranean cooking, enhancing flavors and adding aromatic profiles to dishes. Common herbs and spices used include oregano, basil, thyme, rosemary, parsley, cinnamon, cumin, and paprika.
